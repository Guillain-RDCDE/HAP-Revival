# Code of Conduct

## Short version

Be kind. Be precise. Assume good faith. Don't be a jerk.

We're a small community of people who care about audiophile hardware and open software. There is no room and no time for harassment, gatekeeping, or drama.

## Long version

This project follows the [Contributor Covenant 2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/) in spirit:

- **Respect** all contributors regardless of experience level, audio gear, listening preferences, country, language, age, or background.
- **Disagree on technical merit**, not on personality. "I think this approach is wrong because X" is welcome; "you're wrong" is not.
- **Help newcomers**. The HAP community is tiny — every new contributor is precious.
- **Credit others** for their work, especially in commits and changelog entries.

## What we will not tolerate

- Personal attacks, harassment, doxxing.
- Discriminatory language of any kind.
- Sustained derailing of technical discussions.
- Posting copyrighted material we explicitly exclude (Sony binaries, pirated music, paid service decompiles).

## Reporting

If something happens that needs intervention, contact the project maintainer (currently [@Guillain-RDCDE](https://github.com/Guillain-RDCDE)) directly via GitHub. Reports are treated confidentially.

## Enforcement

The maintainer can:

- Ask a contributor to amend or remove content.
- Lock or close a thread that is no longer productive.
- Revoke commit access for repeated violations.

We hope none of this ever applies.
